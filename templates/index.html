Protein Sequence Data

Protein Sequence Data

Orthology inference is most often based on molecular protein sequences. For a comparison of different orthology prediction methods, a common set of sequences must be established. Therefore, only identical proteins are mapped to each other.

Currently, we support SeqXML and Fasta sequence formats. For Fasta it is expected that each label starts with a unique ID and the species is somehow encoded.

Due to recent changes on the SeqXML and OrthoXML schema, those formats are currently disabled.

Ortholog Relation Data

Ortholog Relation Data

The most general form of specifying orthology is a pairwise listing of orthologous proteins. Therefore, we encourage to upload your prediction in the pairwise format, a simple text file with two tab-separated columns of orthologous protein ids.

Text (pairwise orthologs) XML

Example Datasets

  • A small dataset comprising 7 Bacteria genomes in the pairwise text format: BacExample.txt (273Kb)
  • Eukaryotic dataset with 9 genomes in pairwise text format: EukBBH.txt (5Mb)
